## 1. One-line identity

**A Vancouver building-risk and home-services coordination layer** that helps owners, strata councils, and property managers discover envelope and leakage risk, compare vetted contractor options, and execute projects — **without operating as a contractor or installer**.

**Public positioning:** Building Envelope & Home Services Advisory — Vancouver  
**Internal role:** Project Acquisition Partner — owns deal flow, trust, lead acquisition, coordination  
**Not:** window seller, general contractor, technician, installer

**Tagline candidate:** *Find the risk. Compare trusted options. Coordinate the project.*

## 2. Advisor thesis (source + validation)

Original advisor guidance (Persian) distilled and validated for Vancouver:

### What the advisor got right

| Claim | Vancouver reality |
|-------|-------------------|
| Own **deal flow + trust + coordination**, not installation | Contractors weak at sales, strata process, multi-stakeholder comms; margin sits with who finds problem first and who PM trusts |
| Sell **risk**, not windows | BC retrofit pressure, insurance, moisture, strata liability → urgency; windows are commodity, risk is budget |
| **Strata = primary whale** | 20–200 units, $100K–$multi-M per project, 3–10% commission = $10K–$100K+ per deal |
| **Inspection funnel** → free review → multi-quote | Low-friction entry + 3 competitive bids = trust + commission protection |
| **Restoration cos + property managers** upstream | Restorers see failure first; PMs control access and portfolios |
| **Geographic wedges** | West Van (luxury/custom), North Van (rain/envelope), Burnaby towers (retrofit), Richmond (investor-heavy) |
| **Emergency leak network** | High urgency, dispatch fees, fast commission — wedge product |
| **Community referral** (Persian/Chinese/Indian) | Real but platform must stay **strata-professional** |

### Gaps to design for (advisor underplayed)

1. **Regulation** — coordination/referral vs unlicensed engineering advice; partner licensed inspectors; clear disclaimers  
2. **Strata sales cycle** — 6–18 months for large jobs; need lead fees, retainers, urgent jobs for cash flow  
3. **Commission bypass** — platform must log lead source, quote acceptance, project start  
4. **Quality control** — one bad contractor kills trust; vetting, SLA, post-job documentation  
5. **Trades shortage** — value = scheduling, scope clarity, PM communication  
6. **Seasonality** — diversify revenue streams

## 5. Core products (product roadmap)

### A — Building Envelope Risk Snapshot (conversion engine)

Digital evolution of advisor’s “1-page PDF”:

- Photo upload + questionnaire (condensation, drafts, building age, past leaks)  
- Risk score: low / monitor / act / urgent  
- Rough cost **band** (not a formal quote)  
- Optional paid deep dive with licensed partner  
- CTA: “Request 3 vetted options” or “Schedule coordination call”  

**Analog:** Pure Flow “report after every visit” → our “snapshot after every intake”

### B — Multi-Quote Coordination (money engine)

- Single packaged scope → 3 contractor bids  
- Side-by-side comparison (price, timeline, warranty, references)  
- Strata/council-ready summary  
- Commission: 3–10% or disclosed coordination fee  

### C — Emergency Leak Response Network (speed engine)

- 24–48h triage via restoration partner  
- Dispatch/coordination fee + % on remediation + envelope upsell  
- Target: PMs and strata, not only homeowners  

### D — Strata & PM Partner Program (scale)

- Portfolio dashboard, open issues, quote status  
- Monthly retainer or per-building fee  
- White-label reports for council meetings

## 6. Platform build phases

### Phase 1 — Lead & deal CRM (weeks 1–4)

- Intake: homeowner / strata / PM / emergency  
- Pipeline: New → Snapshot → Quotes → Awarded → In progress → Closed → Commission paid  
- Contractor roster (tier, trade, geo, capacity)  
- **Attribution ID on every lead**  
- Tech: forms + Airtable/Supabase or light custom; public site = front door  

### Phase 2 — Trust layer (weeks 5–10)

- Automated Risk Snapshot PDF  
- Contractor vetting (license, insurance, WorkSafeBC, refs)  
- Post-job completion report  
- Dispute workflow  

### Phase 3 — Marketplace (months 3–6)

- PM/strata portal  
- Contractor bid portal  
- Multi-quote comparison UI  
- SMS/email for urgent leaks  

### Phase 4 — Authority (months 6–12)

- SEO hub (strata envelope, 1980–2005 risks, retrofit)  
- Restoration partner inbound API/webhook  
- Bilingual: EN + 中文 + فارسی (community referral, strata-professional tone)

## 11. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Contractor bypass | Referral agreement; own PM relationship; milestone tied to intro ID |
| Liability from “advice” | Licensed inspector partners; disclaimers; coordination not engineering |
| Long strata cycles | Retainers + leak network + lead fees |
| Bad job destroys trust | Tiered vetting; cap projects per contractor; completion reports |
| Unpaid PM work | Coordination fee on projects >$X; scope limits in contract |
